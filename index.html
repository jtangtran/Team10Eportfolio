Team 10/10 has graciously been given the opportunity to work on the Community Discussion Platform (CDP) project by Nicolas LeBlanc, Executive Director of MyLivingCity. The team consists of Phuong (Jennifer) Tang-Tran, Joshua Sivertson, and Changsin Park, and together they are called Team 10/10 for their beauty, brawn, and brains. The CDP project has been created with the purpose of being an online application for collaborative community discussions regarding civic development and improvements.


The Community Discussion Platform is an online web application that allows patrons to discuss issues and problems and propose ideas and projects for their local community. The application provides users with a list of projects in various stages of development, such as the idea stage, the sponsorship stage, and the active collaboration stage. Users can rate these projects in accordance to how much they agree or disagree with their goals, comment on these projects to express their own opinions and viewpoints on the matter, and create new projects to address issues within their own community.

Meet the Team:

Team 10/10 is filled with incredibly talented people. The team has decided to distribute responsibilities in a way which maximizes each strength:

Because of each individual’s passion and proven abilities, the team is able to share diverse perspectives and tackle more complex problems.


Nic LeBlanc is the current Executive Director of MyLivingCity, a Victoria based non profit organization. Nic has served in the Royal Canadian Navy for 17 years as a Naval Engineering Officer. He has brought his leadership and project management experience to the area of sustainability consulting with his company Network Potential. MyLivingCity has the goal to provide the tools for the community to be able to have conversations on how to transform their city in a sustainable manner.
